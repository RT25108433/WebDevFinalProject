Case Studies

Denmark’s Leadership in Offshore Wind – Denmark has been a pioneer in the wind power industry since its inception, actually creating the first ever wind farm in 1991. Since then, Denmark has become a world leader in clean wind power generation, in large part thanks to continued technological advancement in the industry through manufacturers like Vestas. With these advancements and continued investment in wind power infrastructure, last year it was reported that 59.3% of all electricity generated in Denmark was produced from clean wind energy, the largest proportion of any nation in the world.

China’s High Speed Rail Network – A major component in promoting clean infrastructure development is the introduction of clean, efficient transportation. China has recently emerged as a world leader in this endeavour, having now constructed the largest clean high speed rail network in the world, spanning over 40,000km of rail. This rail network has reduced reliance on flights and petrol cars, leading to a drop in air and road travel CO2 emissions, as well as providing access to transportation through less developed areas of the country. This project has been in large part thanks to advancements in train manufacturing, intelligent construction methods and advanced traffic control management systems.
