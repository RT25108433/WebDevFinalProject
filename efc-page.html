Eco-Friendly Construction

Eco-Friendly Construction Goals – A key goal of SDG 9 is the construction of infrastructure which is not only resilient, but also reliable, resource efficient and environmentally sustainable. This is not just limited to roads and buildings, but also rail networks, water systems and clean energy infrastructure, all built with clean, modern construction practices and sustainable resources, such as recycled steel and green concrete. SDG 9 also emphasises the importance of supporting developing countries in their transition away from outdated, environmentally dirty practices.

Achieving These Goals – Advancements in innovation and technology have made these construction goals more achievable than ever. New sustainable materials, such as the above mentioned recycled steel and green concrete, allow for lower emission construction without sacrificing in resilience and reliability. Inclusion of smart energy solutions like the integration of solar panels works to further reduce emissions produced by construction projects. Developing countries are also supported in this endeavour through the transfer of funding, knowledge and technology which can aid in their transition where they may not be able to do so themselves.
