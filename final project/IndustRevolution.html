Fourth Industiral Revolution

What is the Fourth Industrial Revolution? – The Fourth Industrial Revolution is a term popularised by Klaus Schwab in 2016, broadly used to refer to the rapid technological advancement of industry in the 21st century. A major component of this technological advancement according to its proponents is the trend towards automation, including technologies such as the Internet of Things (IoT), cloud computing, cognitive computing and Artificial Intelligence.

Relevance to SDG 9 – Rapid technological development in the realm of industry, largely thanks to technologies related to the Fourth Industrial Revolution, has created the tools through which the goals of SDG 9 can be achieved. Advancements in automation and robotics allow for efficiency in industry to increase as well as making production lines more flexible, reducing wastage and supporting the sustainable industrialisation target of SDG 9. As well as this, Internet of Things advancements have aided in rolling out 5G digital infrastructure to more and more people, which is another massive goal of SDG 9.
