Clean Manufacturing

Clean Manufacturing Goals – An important component of SDG 9 is the advancement of industrialisation and efficiency in manufacturing, without having adverse effects on the environment in exchange. One of the key indicators of this component is the monitoring and tracking of CO2 emissions per unit of value added to the world’s manufacturing chains. Industry is already one of the key contributors to our carbon footprint on the planet and so lessening that footprint while not sacrificing industrialisation and economic development is a challenging balancing act which must be struck.

How can these goals be achieved? – As stated above, the balancing act between increasing industrial capacity and not having an adverse effect on the environment is a complicated problem. Fortunately, through advancements in industrial technologies and processes, these goals are far more attainable than they might seem. Old and environmentally dirty manufacturing equipment is slowly being replaced with newer, cleaner equipment, as well as improving the energy efficiency of manufacturing lines and implementing pollution control systems to directly reduce CO2 emissions.
