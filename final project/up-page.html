Urban Planning

How Urban Planning relates to SDG 9 – All of the advancements in technologies which can improve the lives of people, both directly and indirectly, mean very little if they are not adequately planned for, especially in an urban setting. SDG 9’s goals again, both directly and indirectly, encourage major population centres to invest in strong infrastructure to improve quality of life for those living there. This can come in the form of public transportation, clean water systems, waste management, energy management and access to robust network infrastructure.

Actions to be taken in Urban Planning – SDG 9’s goals steer major population centres towards the idea of the “Smart City”. Smart cities are characterized by their use of Internet of Things sensors, intelligent transportation systems and usage of real-time data to massively improve the efficiency of cities, reduce congestion and overall improve quality of life. It also encourages careful planning and management of industrial zones to minimise pollution and its effect on residents. With clean, energy efficient manufacturing, these industrial zones can be carefully planned to integrate smoothly into urban environments without causing harm to the local community.
