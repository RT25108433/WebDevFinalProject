What are the Sustainable Development Goals? – The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are a set of 17 plans of global development for peace and prosperity, adopted by all UN member states in 2015 as part of the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development. Each SDG sets certain targets for an area in which member states are expected to progress their development to improve the lives of their citizens. These goals range from tackling poverty, hunger, equality, education and industrial development. Each year a report on the global progress of the SDGs is published and presented by the UN Secretary General.

What is Sustainable Development Goal 9? – Sustainable Development Goal 9, “Industry, Innovation, Infrastructure”, focuses on building reliant infrastructure, sustainable industrialization and fostering innovation among member states. SDG 9 consists of 8 targets, split among outcome targets and implementation targets. Currently, a significant target of SDG 9 is to improve internet infrastructure in developing countries by increasing access to the 5G network.
